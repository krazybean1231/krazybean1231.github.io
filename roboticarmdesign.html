6-DOF Robotic Arm

Designed a 6 axis robotic arm myself using 9g servos to motorize the joins.

Author

Evan Yang

Date

Dec 20, 2025

Date Published

Jan 4, 2026

I started to lift weights seriously in the first semester of 10th grade, and protein intake is a very important part of growing muscle. I wanted to make a robotic arm to automate the process of stirring the protein powder, since it take a long time to dissolved everything.

36 Tooth Gear

Starting off with the base of the Robotic Arm, the robotic arm must have a swivel. Since I will be using servos to control all movements of the arm, and the servos I have are limited to 180 degree spin, I need to make gears for a 2:1 ratio, so that the arm has full 360 degree spin.

First I used CAD to make a 36 tooth gear, which is the gear that is going to attach directly to the servo.

The first design of the gear did not account for the width of 3d printer nozzle diameter, and was only given 0.2 mm of space. The M2 screws had a very tight fit with the hole.

The nuts that come with the m2 screws did not fit either.

Had to increase the spacing between the base holes and servo holes by .5 mm since the gears were meshing straight.

The servo is held down by a brace:

The base originally was .3mm to short on the bottom, so a bit of the servo's bottom was sticking out, contributing to the gears not aligning.

The housing will also contain magnets to attach the other parts of the housing like the electronics housing and a top.

Although after printing I realized I failed to account for the top magnets. I decided that I will not print a new housing, and find a cheaper alternative.
