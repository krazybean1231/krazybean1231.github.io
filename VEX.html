DIY FPV Drone

A FPV (first person view) drone assembled from seperately sourced parts.

Author

Evan Yang

Date

Oct 10, 2024

Date Published

Nov 11, 2024

Over the summer of 2024, I got obsessed with FPV drone footage, dreaming of being able to pilot one of these one day. But with any drone pilot comes a drone. Upon researching the options I had for FPV, the results came back strikingly expensive.

Watching more video led me down a rabbit hole of DIY FPV and extensive watch hours mentally prepared me for the large task of creating a fully functional drone. The video on a 7 inch FPV inspired me to source my parts due to its cheaper price. https://youtu.be/0jOUTYBneVo.

Materials

  • Video Transmitter (RUSH Solo Tank 5.8G)

  • 4 BLDC Drone Motors (1300kv 2S-6S)

  • M3 Screws

  • 3s Lipo Battery (11.1 V)

  • 4 7X4X3 propellors

  • 5.8G Antenna (Rush Cherry SMA-LHCP-160mm)

  • ELRS receiver

  • Camera (Runcam Phoenix 2 SE V2)

  • Mark 4 7 inch drone frame

  • F4 V3S Flight controller

  • 45A ESC

  • Soldering Materials

I sourced all of these parts on AliExpress, totaling around $200.

Building It

Building this was not very straightforward. The video by Hoarder Sam did not show the entire built process and a lot of the way the parts were put together I had to figure out myself. This was also my first time soldering, so I had to learn how to solder as well.

These were 6 pads on the ESC used to control the motors where I soldered them myself. Not the best soldering job, but it there's not shorting!

Configuration

After completing building the drone and pairing the controller with the ELRS receiver, I had to use betaflight to configure some settings. Connecting the flightstack to the controller proved to be a challenge because it wouldn’t just directly connect. I had to configure a lot of settings to make sure they matched the hardware of the F4V3 flight stack as well as update the firmware. Here is what the motor configuratation looked like:

(Source: Betaflight) Interface where I configured the directions of the motors to make sure the drone is able to fly properly and properllor direction is also correct.

Testing and Results

Testing the drone went pretty well. The drone was very powerful and I had to make sure I didn’t crash it into anything. The done did fly, but since I don’t have FPV goggles yet, I wasn’t confident enough to control the drone as seen in the FPV videos.

Wanted to be safe since this was indoors, but the drone works!

2025 Update: My friend wanted to build a drone as well, so I referred this model to him and helped him build a near identical one. This drone build was pretty successful.
